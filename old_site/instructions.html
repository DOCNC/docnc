IMPORTANT INFORMATION CONCERNING USE OF
THE DARTMOUTH CABIN AT DONNER SUMMIT

This letter confirms that you will be using the cabin from noon on DATE1 to noon on DATE2 with NUM people. For these dates, your use is non-exclusive.

The combination of the cabin is COMBO. The lock is similar to a briefcase lock. To open the lock, set it to the number above, depress the shackle, and then pull it open. Upon departure, the lock needs to be snapped shut; it will NOT close properly if the combination is showing.

We all appreciate a spotlessly clean cabin, so please be sure to leave it a bit tidier than when you arrived. There is a cabin condition form included with this letter; please complete it and return it to me. A cabin left untidy will result in a $90 charge. The person who follows you decides whether or not to assess this fee based upon the condition of the cabin when they arrive. Here are a few points to help:

A. POWER

1. Upon arrival

If you arrive at night, be certain to bring a powerful flashlight. After entering, go to the far left corner (northwest or to the left of the wood stove) of the cabin. Find the large circuit breaker, and switch it on.

2. Upon Departure

Reverse procedure above. Be certain to touch no other switches as they provide power to keep the pump room warm and the water lines from freezing when no one is around.

B. WATER

See the special note attached to the hot water heater to the left of the kitchen sink. The hot water requires special attention, so take your time reading the instructions. Winter water procedures are complex; please read the enclosed Water Procedures.

C. WOOD

Our wood is very expensive and should be used sparingly. In 1990, wood averaged over $150/cord. Please distinguish carefully between the various types of wood:

1. Oak, to be used in the pot-belly wood stove ONLY. This is by far the heaviest.

2. Barkless tamarack, to be used in the wood-burning kitchen stove.

3. Tamarack with bark, to be used in the fireplace.

4. Cedar, to be used in the pot-belly stove.

Please be certain to restock the wood you use from the supplies in the basement. All wood splitting should be done on a wooden base, not on the hearth next to the fireplace.

D. ASHES

Please take extreme care with the ashes and coals when leaving the cabin. Ashes and coals should be placed in the receptacle in the outside porch. It is a steel garbage can with a red top.

Never throw the ashes over the railing. In addition to leaving unsightly scars, the ashes may burn through plastic pipes and cause methane fires from the holding tank. The cabin will burn very quickly should there be a methane fire.

E. PARKING

During the winter, because of the snow blocking the roadway to the cabin, you will need to obtain a parking permit for your car's windshield from the Alpine Skills Institute (ASI). In order to park at ASI, please give them the attached form(s), one per car. Remember, we have paid for three parking places; additional spaces may be available for a fee from ASI. Please park only where they indicate. If no one is at the desk, be sure to sign the register! Cars will be towed if these guidelines are not followed. Each winter, several unfortunate car owners must retrieve their towed cars for $200 each!

F. HEAT

1. Upstairs and Bathroom

There are portable electric heaters upstairs and in the bathroom. Under no circumstances should these heaters be left on when they are unattended, even if only for a minute.

2. Wood Heating Stove in Living Room

The wood stove is very efficient and can heat the whole cabin. An instruction pamphlet has been left at the cabin. In summary, only barkless tamarack, cedar, and oak should be burned. When burning softwood, the opening on the door of the stove should be set at six or higher. The higher the number of the opening on the door, the hotter the fire, so you can adjust the stove accordingly. Softwood, particularly the bark, is high in tar and resin content and must be burned at high temperatures so it will not accumulate in the chimney and cause a fire in the roof and walls. At night, and for extended burns, use oak wood at setting five or higher.

G. TOILET

1. Arrival

The toilet must be primed before use. The steps are:

a. Make sure the plunger handle is pushed in under the toilet bowl. It is located on the base of the toilet as you face it, and it sticks out about four inches when not pushed in.

b. Pour three gallons of water into the toilet. This should fill the toilet to the C level on the scale on the front base above the plunger. C means charged or primed. F means full!

c. Pour 8 ounces (one packet) of Aqua-chem into the toilet.

d. As long as there is power running to the toilet, you may flush the toilet by pushing the button at the top left of the back of the toilet. No power? Check to see if the red light is on the black box behind the toilet. If it is not, punch the red (thin) stick in on the back end of the box. Now it should work!

2. Departure

Pull the plunger out to drain the toilet into the holding tank below. Then push it in again, firmly. When pushed in, the plunger can be quite hard to pull out, so be patient and carefully pull it out. Please do not kick or move the toilet - if you do, the toilet seal will be broken and sewage will pour out onto your feet!

H. REFRIGERATOR

1. Arrival

Plug it in and close the door. The plug is on the left side between the cabinet and the refrigerator.

2. Departure

Remove all perishable food, unplug, clean , and leave the door open.

I. GARBAGE

The basic rule is: anything you bring in should be taken back out with you. This rule applies all year round.

Handle the garbage this way:

1. Sort the burnable rubbish from the non-burnable material (you may also sort out recyclable materials).

2. Burn what you can in the fireplace or stove.

3. Place the rest in garbage bags and take it with you. Please recycle what you can.

NOTE: We no longer have access to the dumpster at Sugar Bowl Academy. Please take all trash out of the cabin and dispose of it properly. DO NOT leave your trash on or near the SBA dumpster. DO NOT leave your trash at the cabin. Please pack it all out. Thank you!

J. CLEANING

Upon departure make sure you have washed all the dishes and swept or vacuumed all floors. Clean excess ashes out of the fireplace and the wood stove as instructed above.

K. MISCELLANEOUS

1. Operation of Wood Cooking Stove in Kitchen

a. Empty ash pan before starting fire.

b. Damper in stove pipe behind stove should be in vertical position.

c. Ash box damper on left side of stove should be open.

d. Start fire, using wood cut to size for the stove (stored behind cellar stairs).

e. To control heat, damper in back of stove may be partially closed.

f. Ash box damper may be closed when the fire is burning well.

2. Operation of Fireplace

a. Be sure damper is open before starting fire.

b. ALWAYS keep fire screen in front of the fireplace, except when feeding the fire. Once a fire in the cabin starts, the chances of putting it out are poor. Familiarize yourself with the location of the fire extinguishers and exits.

c. Put wood and burnables well back into the fireplace. This reduces the risk of a cabin fire and produces less smoke. d. When leaving the cabin, be sure any fire is safe to leave unattended. Place the screen tightly in front of the fireplace.

3. Wood Supply

a. Firewood is located in the basement and is reached by climbing down the trap door in the mudroom (next to the bathroom). The light cord is located above the stairs to the cellar.

b. All wood chopping must be done in the basement. Wood should NOT be chopped on the hearth.

c. Cook stove wood (short pieces) is stored behind the basement stairs. Firewood for the fireplace and heating stove is stored under the living room.

d. Before leaving the cabin, restore the firewood supply to the white line (a cord) on the wall next to the fireplace.

e. The various types of wood are usually labeled. Wood is very expensive. Please use sparingly.

4. Emergencies

a. As of Fall 1993 the cabin has a phone. It is was installed for use primarily in emergency situations, and will only make local calls. Please use it in the spirit that was intended, as many people go up to the cabin to 'escape'. If you need to make a long distance call, please use your calling card or make the call collect. Any toll calls charged to the cabin phone will be billed to you along with a phone misuse charge. The cabin's phone number is 530-426-0413.

Most of all, enjoy the cabin! Please add an account of your stay to the log book for others to share!
